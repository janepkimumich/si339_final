Frosted Sugar Cookies

Frosted Sugar Cookies

Ingredients:

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, beat the butter and sugar until smooth. Add the egg, vanilla extract, and almond extract, and beat until combined.
  2. Beat in flour and salt until completely combined. Beat in 1.5 Tbsp of milk, adding until creamy.
  3. Spoon dough onto lined baking sheet 3 inches apart. Chill in refrigerator for 20-30 minutes and preheat oven to 350°F.
  4. Bake chilled cookies for 12-15 mintues or until lightly browned. Remove from oven and allow to cool for 5 minutes.
  5. Optional: Frost cooled cookies as desired.

Brownie Cookies

Brownie Cookies

Ingredients:

Instructions:

  1. Melt chocolate, set aside to slightly cool.
  2. Whisk flour, cocoa powder, espresso powder, baking powder, and salt together. Set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, beat butter, brown sufar, and granulated sufar together until smooth and creamy. Add the eggs and vanilla extract and beat together. Pour in the cooled melted chocolate and mix.
  4. Pour in the dry ingredients and beat until combined. Beat in chocolate chips.
  5. Preheat overn to 350°F. Cover and chill dough while the oven preheats. Chilling longer creates thicker cookies.
  6. Scoop dough into 1.5 Tbsp balls and place 3 inches apart. Bake for 12-13 minutes or until the edges appear set.
  7. Remove from the oven and allow cookies to cool for 5 minutes. Cookies stay fresh covered at room temperature for up to 1 week.

Chocolate Chip

Chocolate Chip Cookies

Ingredients:

Instructions:

  1. Combine butter, brown sugar, white sugar, vanilla extract, salt, baking powder, and baking soda. Beat until soft, fluffy, and pale.
  2. Add eggs one at a time, letting each incorporate fully before adding the next. Add flour all at once. Once incorporated, add chocolate chips until dough is homogenous.
  3. Divide dough into 8 equal portions. Round into balls, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate at least 12 hours before baking.
  4. Pregeat oven to 350°F. Leave ample space between dough portions. Sprinkle salt to taste.
  5. Bake for 20-22 minutes, or until puffed and lightly brown. Cool cookies on baking sheet before serving.

Snickerdoodle

Snickerdoodle Cookies

Ingredients:

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F
  2. In a large bowl, mix butter, eggs, sugar, and vanilla extract until combined.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine flour, cream of tartar, baking soda, and salt.
  4. Blend dry ingredients into large bowl.
  5. Chill dough for 10-15 minutes while oven preheats. Mix granulated sugar and cinnamon in a small bowl.
  6. Scoop dough into 1 Tbsp balls and coat in cinnamon sugar. Place on cookie sheet and bake for 10 minutes, or until edges brown.

Cookies and Cream

Cookies and Cream Cookies

Ingredients:

Instructions:

  1. Whisk flour, cornstarch, salt, and baking soda in a medium bowl. Set aside.
  2. Cream butter and cream cheese together. Add granulated and brown sugar in and beat until light and fluffy. Add the egg and vanilla in and mix until combined.
  3. Add the dry mixture into the wet and mix on low until almost combined. Break Oreos into large pieces and fold in with 1/2 cup white chocolate chips until evenly distributed. Cover and chill for at least an hour.
  4. Preheat oven to 350°F. Portion out 2 Tbsp balls of dough and place on sheet about 2 inches apart. Press remaining white chooclate chips onto the tops of cookies and bake for about 11 minutes or until edges are set and just turning golden.

Matcha Cookies

Matcha Cookies

Ingredients:

Instructions:

  1. In one bowl, whisk together the flour, cornstarch, salt, and baking powder.
  2. In a separate bowl, beat together butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy. Drizze in milk and mix until creamy.
  3. Gradually add dry ingredients. Pour in chocolate chips and mix until combined.
  4. Preheat oven to 400°F and chill dough in refrigerator while oven preheats.
  5. Scoop dough into 8 even balls and evenly space out on baking sheet. Bake for 12-14 minutes or until edges appear slightly crisp.

Lotus Biscoff Cookies

Lotus Biscoff Cookies

Ingredients:

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. In a food procesor, pulse 16 of the Biscoff cookies until you have 1 cup of fine crumbs and set aside. Break up another 8 Biscoff cookies into chunks and set aside. Reserve the remaining 8 cookies for decorating the tops of cookies at the end.
  3. In a large bowl, cream the butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar. Add eggs and vanilla and beat again. Add cookie butter and mix until combined.
  4. Add the flour, finely crushed Biscoff cookie crumbs, cornstarch, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. Mix until combined.
  5. Stir in broken up Biscoff cookies and white chocolate chips.
  6. Scoop dough into evenly sized balls and space cookies a few inches apart on baking sheets. Bake for 8-10 minutes or until edges are just set.
  7. Remove from oven and press half of reserved Biscoff cookies into the top of each cookie along with additional chocolate chips. Let cool on baking sheets for 10 minutes before transferring.

Palmier Cookies

Palmier Cookies

Ingredients:

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together sugar and cinnamon. Set aside.
  2. Divide puff pastry into two even pieces. Leave one refrigerated to keep cool.
  3. Roll the other half into a 11x15 rectangle. Lightly brush the surface of the dough with melted butter and evenly sprinkle with the cinnamon sugar. Use the rolling pin to gently roll over to help press the mixture into the pastry.
  4. Starting with the shorter ends, fold dough over itself towards the center, leacing a 2in gap between the two pieces of dough.
  5. Brush with more butter and sprinkle with cinnamon sugar. Continue to fold dough. Wrap in plastic wrap and chill in refrigerator for at least 1 hour.
  6. Preheat oven to 415°F Remove dough from refrigerator and use a sharp knife to cut slices into 1/2in slices.
  7. Place on baking sheet and bake for 13-15 minutes or until golden brown. Allow to cool completely on baking sheet.

Jam Cookies

Jam Cookies

Ingredients:

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, sift together flour, cornstarch and salt.
  2. In another bowl, beat together butter and lemon zest until light. Add the granulated sugar and beat until completely incorporated. Add whole egg and egg yolk and beat until light and fluffy. Add in dry ingredients and beat until just incorporated. Wrap dough and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  3. Preheat oven to 350°F. Roll out dough 1/8 in thick. Use a cookie cutter to cut out cookies. Transfer cookies to baking sheets, 1/2 in apart. Bake for 10 minutes or until edges are golden.
  4. Spread jam on the bottoms of half the cookies, spreading lightly. Top with remaining cookies, bottoms down. Dust with powdered sugar. Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 1 week.

Gingerbread Cookies

Gingerbread Cookies

Ingredients:

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, salt, ginger, cinnamon, allspice, and cloves. Set aside.
  2. In another bowl, beat together butter, brown sugar, and molasses until combined and creamy. Beat in egg and vanilla.
  3. Add dry ingredients to wet ingredients and beat until combined. Divide dough in half, wrap, and chill for at least 3 hours.
  4. Preheat oven to 350°F. Flour a work surface and roll out dough until 1/4in thick. Cut out into shapes.
  5. Place dough on baking sheets, 1in apart. Bake for 9-10 minutes or shorter for smaller shapes. Cool for 5 minutes on baking sheet before transferring. Decorate as desired.
